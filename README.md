# Mobile Device Hardware Support Case Study: Power Issues

---

##  Overview (TL;DR)

An iPhone device was experiencing rapid battery drain during normal use and intermittent charging failure through the Lightning port. Investigation included reviewing Battery Health in iOS Settings and testing the charging port with a known-good cable.

The root cause was identified as:

- A degraded battery requiring replacement  
- A worn Lightning connector flex cable  

Replacing both components restored normal portable operation and system stability.

---

##  Background / Context

The user reported:

- Battery percentage dropping faster than usual  
- Charging cable not working when plugged into the phone  

---

##  Problem Statement

- Battery capacity reported **below 80%** in Battery Health  
- Lightning connector port worn and unable to charge reliably  

---

## Environment & Tools

### Device Information

- **Device:** iPhone XR  
- **OS:** iOS  

### Hardware Components

- 2,942 mAh Lithium-ion Battery  
- Charging Port & Microphone Flex Cable  
- Display Assembly Adhesive  

### Tools / Utilities Used

- Heating Pack  
- Tweezers  
- Opening Picks  
- P2 Pentalobe Screwdriver  
- Phillips #000 Screwdriver  
- Tri-point Y000 Screwdriver  
- Standoff Screwdriver  
- Suction Pump Opening Tool  

---

##  Troubleshooting Process

1. Verified Lightning port behavior using a known-good cable.  
2. Observed no immediate charging response unless the connector was adjusted.  
3. Checked Battery Health within iOS Settings.  
4. Confirmed battery capacity at **75%**, indicating degradation.  
5. Tested device using known-good replacement battery and flex cable.  
6. Observed stable battery operation and normal charging.  
7. Confirmed original battery and flex cable as failure points.

---

##  Root Cause

- **Battery:** Degraded cells caused rapid power loss and instability.  
- **Lightning Flex Cable:** Port wear caused inconsistent charging detection.

---

##  Resolution

- Replaced internal battery  
- Replaced Lightning connector flex cable  
- Performed post-repair validation testing  

The device maintained stable operation on both battery and charging power with no further failures.

---

##  Results / Impact

-  Normal battery performance restored  
-  Reliable cable detection restored  
-  Full mobile usability returned to end user  
-  Reduced risk of unexpected shutdowns  

---

##  Lessons Learned

- Battery Health in iOS is a key indicator for battery replacement decisions.  
- Lightning flex assemblies are fragile and prone to damage from bending or strain.  
- Always confirm faults using known-good components before replacement.
